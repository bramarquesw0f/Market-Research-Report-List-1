Ultrasound provides a non-invasive method for soft tissue evaluation, while Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) offers detailed imaging of organs and tissues without radiation exposure. Computed Tomography (CT) enables rapid imaging, critical for trauma cases, though it involves ionizing radiation. X-rays are commonly used for assessing skeletal injuries and conditions. Other modalities may include nuclear medicine and fluoroscopy, catering to specific diagnostic needs in pediatric care.</p></p>
