<p><p>The Interstitial Free (IF) sheet steel market comprises primarily two types: hot rolled and cold rolled. Hot rolled IF sheet steel is produced at elevated temperatures, allowing for easier shaping and forming, and is typically thicker and stronger. In contrast, cold rolled IF sheet steel is processed at room temperature, resulting in a smoother surface and greater dimensional accuracy, making it suitable for applications requiring precision. Both types are favored in automotive and manufacturing industries for their excellent mechanical properties and weldability.</p></p>
