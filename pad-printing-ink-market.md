<p><p>The pad printing ink market comprises several types: One-component ink is ready to use and requires no mixing, ideal for quick applications. Two-component ink involves a hardener for enhanced durability and is often used in industrial settings. Dry ink is a powdered form that requires a solvent, providing versatile application options. Oxidation ink dries through air exposure, suitable for various substrates. Sublimation ink transfers vibrant images onto materials via heat. Special inks cater to unique needs, such as glow-in-the-dark or metallic finishes.</p></p>
